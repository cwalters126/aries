Minolta SRT 102

I started seeing a lot of cameras on Facebook Marketplace and got to researching. I learned about point and shoot, SLR, aperture and ISO ratings, the price of film, etc (still not sure I understand how rangerfinder cameras work). I decided I wanted an SLR, because it was something to grow with. I ended up looking for old Minolta SRT cameras, and deep dove into the r/MINOLTA GANG "Fairly Frequently Asked Questions" page. It was the most organized, useful, informative reddit group I had ever seen.

Eventually, I purchased this Minolta SRT 102 from my new friend, Dennis. I had some work ahead of me. I replaced the top plate with one that was in better condition, and tracked down some batteries (not the mercury ones!) in order to use the exposure meter. I made my own camera strap out of some rainbow canvas material I got at Field's Fabrics, and I ordered a lens cap that fit.

I have been using FujiFilm 400 color negative (35 mm) film since using the camera. I was very pleased with the colors, especially the ones I got back from Olympic National Park. I ended up getting my dad the same camera (back to Dennis!) because he had the same camera when he was a teen!

In November 2025, some friends and Elisa and I will be traveling to Scotland! I will bring my camera, and I will be trying out a new film! Lomography Color Negative 800 ISO film. 800 ISO is good for low light situations, and I am excited to see how they look.

If anyone is interested, they can view my ameteur film photos on the photos page!
