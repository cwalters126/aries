Sears Portable with Correction Typewriter

Cleaned up this Sears Typewriter I got off facebook marketplace. Replaced the ribbon (they sell purple ink!!). Aesthetically, it fits perfectly in my craft room, but I actually use it so much I keep it in my office now.

This has been super fun for me to have around the house. I use it for letters, notes, stationery, my random little projects, etc.

A couple things I learned: this machine uses a lowercase 'l' as a 1. There is no 'plus' (+) sign, supposedly you can use a combination of dashes to create one, but not sure I have achieved that. The exclaimation point (!) uses similar logic; you use a period and the apostrophe.

I have been using the typewriter consistenty for journaling. Elisa has boxes of journals from throughout her life and it inspired me to try again. It's a lot easier to write when I am not smearing ink down the side of my hand...It's a good way to avoid having a printer in the house for my little projects. I've even done a few poems on it too. I have a theory that this is why Russ uses so many + in his poems; I can't fingure out how to make a + on this typewriter. I think he does it as a sort of 'f you' to the hipster poets with typewriters, but I guess I will never know for sure.
